Go to canva.com/signup — sign in with your Google account for the fastest setup.

Once logged in, you can go directly to the Canva Code tool:

Open Canva Code

What is “Vibe Coding”?

Vibe coding is a way of creating software by describing what you want in everyday language, rather than writing technical code yourself. The term was coined by AI researcher Andrej Karpathy in early 2025, and was named Collins Dictionary · Word of the Year 2025

Here is how it works: you describe what you want (“build me a card gallery with filters”), the AI generates the code, you inspect the result to see if it matches your vision, and then you refine it by describing what to change. No programming skills needed.

Think of it like ordering at a restaurant — you describe what you want, the kitchen does the work. If the dish is not quite right, you send it back with feedback.

Describe → Generate → Inspect → Refine

// the scope

We are not building production software.
We are creating lightweight, interactive teaching and presentation aids.

The goal is simple: take something that would normally be a static slide — a list, a chart, a comparison — and turn it into something your audience can click, filter, and explore. These are small, useful tools, not full applications.

The “Enthusiastic Intern” Rule

Think of Canva Code as a very eager but inexperienced assistant. It does brilliantly with clear, simple tasks — but struggles when you ask for too much at once.

“Build a 4-card comparison tool”

Clear task → great result

“Build a 50-variable simulator”

Too complex → blank screen

Keep it small. Keep it useful. Keep it stable.

If your first attempt does not work, the fix is almost always the same: make the prompt simpler. Remove features. Focus on one thing at a time.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to create your first interactive component:

  1. Open canva.com and log in to your account.

  2. Click on the Canva AI text box on the homepage. At the bottom of the text box, click the Code button.

  3. Go to the Choose Your Track section above. Copy the prompt template for your chosen track.

  4. Paste the prompt into the Code text box. Feel free to edit it — change the topic, the number of items, or the style. Then press Submit.

  5. Wait a few seconds. Canva will generate a working interactive component. Click around — test the buttons, filters, and interactions.

  6. Not happy? Type a follow-up instruction describing what to change (see the Inspect & Refine section below).

  7. When you are satisfied, click “Use in a design” to embed the component into your Canva presentation slide.

Common mistake: Do not type directly into the Canva AI text box and press Enter — that creates AI-generated designs, not code. Always click the </> Code button first.

Inspect & Refine

After generating your component, two things can happen. Either something is broken and needs fixing, or everything works but you want to improve it. Either way, the process is the same: describe the change in plain English.

Fix something

“The filter button does not work. Please fix it. Keep everything else the same.”

Improve it

“Make the text larger for a presentation and change the colors to blue and grey.”

You can refine as many times as you like. Each time, the AI updates the existing component rather than starting over.

The “Interactive Sandwich”

When you put your component into a presentation, do not just drop it onto a blank slide. Give it context. Frame it like a sandwich with three layers:

Setup — Tell your audience why they are interacting
Your Widget — The interactive component
Synthesis — What is the takeaway?

Example: “Adjust the interest rate below — what happens after 30 years?”

Prompt Cheat Sheet

A good prompt has four elements. The more specific you are, the better the result:

Purpose

“for a university presentation”

Elements

“6 cards with title, summary, and tags”

Behavior

“when clicked, show a detail view”

Style

“clean, professional design”

Common Refinement Prompts

Copy and adapt these when you need to fix or improve your component:

Fix a bug

“The filter button does not work. Please fix it. Keep everything else.”

Change style

“Make the text larger and change the colors to blue and grey.”

Add a feature

“Add a search box at the top that filters cards by title.”

Simplify

“Remove the animations. Keep the layout clean and simple.”

Interaction ≠ Understanding

AI-generated components can look very professional, but that does not mean the content inside them is correct. Always verify facts, formulas, and data with your own expertise before presenting to an audience.

Good for

Low-stakes exploration, teaching aids, prototypes

Not for

Clinical decisions, legal advice, graded assessments

When to Use What

Not everything needs Canva Code. Canva already has built-in features for simple interactions:

Canva Native Features

  • Polls & Q&A (Canva Live)
  • Quizzes
  • Forms & surveys
  • Standard templates

Canva Code (Vibe Coding)

  • Custom explorers
  • Variable simulators
  • Unique data showcases
  • Anything templates can’t do
